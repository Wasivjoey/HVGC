1. What is HVGC LINEUP?

HVGC LINEUP is the scheduling system for your church's ministry teams. It lets volunteers say when they're available, see which services they're on, swap roles when they can't make it, and complete the training required to serve. Administrators use it to build the team, assign roles and training, create services, and schedule people.

There are two sides to the app: the Volunteer side (everyone has this) and the Administration side (only admins see this). Both are reached from the menu on the left.

2. Creating an account & signing in

New accounts go through two checks before they can be used:

  1. Go to the sign-in page and click “Create an account.”
  2. Enter your name, a username, email, optional phone, and a password (at least 6 characters). Click Create account.
  3. Verify your email. Open the verification link sent to your address (if email isn't set up on the server, the link is shown on screen and on the admin's page).
  4. Wait for approval. An administrator approves new accounts. You'll be able to sign in once your email is verified and an admin has approved you.
  5. Then sign in with your username or email plus your password. Use Sign out (bottom-left) when you're done.

Forgot your password? Click “Forgot your password?” on the sign-in page, enter your username or email, and follow the reset link (it's emailed, or shown on screen when email isn't configured). The link expires after one hour.

Tip: the very first account on a brand-new system becomes an approved administrator automatically, so the team can get set up.

3. Finding your way around

The dark menu on the left is always visible. It is split into sections:

  • Volunteer — Dashboard, My Availability, Services, Role Swaps, Training, My Profile, and this Manual.
  • Administration — only shown to admins: Admin Home, People, Roles, Trainings, Schedule, Availability Grid, Swap Approvals.

Your name and role (Volunteer or Administrator) appear at the bottom, with the Sign out button. Coloured messages appear at the top of the page to confirm actions or warn you about something.

4. Your dashboard

The dashboard (Dashboard in the menu) is your home base. It shows:

  • Training to complete — any required training you must finish before you can serve (highlighted at the top if outstanding).
  • Your upcoming assignments — services you're scheduled for, with a status badge (Scheduled / Confirmed / Declined).
  • Your roles & qualification — each role you hold and whether you're fully qualified or still in training.
  • Swaps you can cover — open swap requests from teammates that you're qualified to pick up.

5. Setting your availability

Open My Availability. This is how schedulers know when to use you.

  1. Choose ✅ Available or ⛔ Unavailable.
  2. Pick a From date. To mark just one day, leave To date blank. To mark a stretch of days (for example a holiday), set a To date as well — every day in the range is marked at once.
  3. Add an optional note (e.g. “available after 11am”) and click Save availability.

Use Quick mark upcoming Sundays to flag the next eight Sundays available in one click each. Your marked dates are listed on the right; use Remove to clear any entry. Saving the same date again updates it.

6. Services & the roster

Open Services to see upcoming (and recent past) service times. Click Open on any service to view its detail page, which shows:

  • The date, start time, and location.
  • Any service notes and the 📄 programme flow document (click to open/download it) if the admin attached one.
  • The full roster — who is serving in each role.

7. Confirming or declining an assignment

When you're scheduled for a service, open that service. Under “Your role this service” you can:

  • Confirm — let the team know you'll be there.
  • Decline — flag that you can't make it.
  • Request swap — see the next section.

8. Role swaps

Can't make a service you're scheduled for? Hand it off:

  1. On the service page, open Request swap under your role, add an optional reason, and post it.
  2. Qualified teammates now see your slot under Role Swaps and on their dashboard. One of them clicks Volunteer to cover it.
  3. An administrator approves the swap, which reassigns the slot to the volunteer. You'll see the status change to Approved.

The Role Swaps page has two lists: Swaps you can cover (only roles you're qualified for) and Your swap requests (with their status; you can cancel one that's still open).

9. Service notes

On any service page, the 📝 Notes panel lets anyone on the team leave a note about that service time (reminders, special instructions, etc.). Type your note and click Add note. You can delete notes you wrote.

10. Training & videos

Open Training to see everything assigned to you and whether it's To do or Completed. Click Start to open a training, where you'll find:

  • A 🎬 training video (if one is attached). Important: the video automatically pauses the moment you switch tabs or leave the window, so you need to actually watch it.
  • Written content / instructions.
  • A “Mark this training complete” button — click it once you've finished.

You must complete every required training for a role before you can be scheduled in it (see section 20).

11. Your profile

My Profile lets you update your name and phone number, and shows the roles you hold with your qualification status for each. Your email and account type are shown but managed by an administrator.

12. Admin home Admins only

Sections 12–19 are visible only to administrators. Admin Home gives an overview: counts of people, roles, services, trainings, open swaps, and training in progress, plus your next services and any swaps needing attention.

13. Managing people

New sign-ups appear on Admin Home and on People with a Pending badge — click Approve to let them sign in. Open People for the full team list, then Manage on anyone to:

  • Approve / revoke approval, and mark email verified manually (or copy the verification link to send them).
  • Assign / remove roles. Assigning a role automatically queues that role's required training for the person.
  • Assign, complete, reset, or remove training.
  • Make admin / revoke admin, and activate / deactivate the account. (You can't change your own admin/approval status.)

14. Roles

Open Roles to create the serving positions on your team (Audio Engineer, Lighting, Camera, etc.). For each role you can edit its name and description, or delete it. Each role shows how many people are assigned and how many are fully qualified.

15. Building training

Open Trainings to create training modules. For each one you set:

  • Title and short description.
  • Video link — paste a YouTube, Vimeo, or direct .mp4 URL; it embeds on the volunteer's training page and auto-pauses when they leave.
  • Linked role — which role this training belongs to.
  • Content / instructions — the written material.
  • Required to serve — tick this if it must be completed before serving in the linked role.

You can edit or delete trainings, and each shows how many people have completed it.

16. Services & documents

Open Schedule. The + New service form creates a service with a title, date, start time, location, notes, and an optional programme flow document (PDF, Word, PowerPoint, Excel, images). You can later edit any of these — including changing the time and location — and attach, replace, or remove the document from the service's scheduling page.

17. The scheduling board

Click Schedule on a service to build its team. For each role you see every candidate with two signals:

  • A coloured dot for availability — available, unavailable, not marked — on that service's date.
  • A badge showing whether they're Qualified or Not trained.

Click Assign to schedule a qualified person. People who haven't finished the required training are blocked, but you can use Override to assign them anyway. The current roster is shown at the top, where you can Remove anyone.

18. Availability grid

Open Availability Grid for an at-a-glance matrix of every volunteer against each upcoming service date, using the same green / red / grey dots. It's the fastest way to see who's free before you schedule.

19. Swap approvals

Open Swap Approvals to see all swap requests. When a teammate has volunteered to cover a slot, the request shows Ready to approve — click Approve to reassign the slot to them, or Reject to decline. Open requests with no volunteer yet can also be rejected.

20. The qualification rule

This is the heart of the system: a volunteer can only be scheduled for a role once they have completed every required training linked to that role.

  • Assigning a role auto-queues its required training.
  • Until the training is done, the person shows as “Training X/Y” and can't be assigned on the board (admins may override).
  • The same rule decides which open swaps a volunteer is allowed to cover.

21. Quick FAQ

I can't be assigned to a role. You probably haven't finished its required training — check the Training page.

The training video stopped. That's intentional — it pauses when you leave the window. Return to the tab and press play to resume.

I marked a whole week off but it shows many rows. A date range is stored as one entry per day; that's normal and lets schedulers see each day.

I just registered but can't sign in. Make sure you've opened the email verification link, then wait for an administrator to approve your account — both are required.

I can't see the admin menu. Those tools are for administrators. Ask an existing admin to grant you access from the People page.

I forgot to confirm and the service passed. No problem — confirming is a courtesy so the team knows you're coming; it doesn't remove you from the roster.
