## Getting started

### 1. What is HVGC LINEUP?
HVGC LINEUP is the scheduling system for the audio / visual (AV) team. Volunteers
say when they're available, see which services they're on, swap roles when they
can't make it, and complete the training required to serve. Administrators build
the team, assign roles and training, create services, and schedule people.

There are two sides: the **Volunteer** side (everyone has this) and the
**Administration** side (admins only). Both are reached from the left-hand menu.

### 2. Creating an account & signing in
New accounts go through two checks before they can be used:
1. On the sign-in page, click **“Create an account.”**
2. Enter your name, a **username**, email, optional phone, and a password (6+
   characters), then click **Create account**.
3. **Verify your email** via the link sent to your address (if email isn't set up
   on the server, the link is shown on screen and on the admin's page).
4. **Wait for approval** — an administrator approves new accounts. You can sign in
   once your email is verified *and* an admin has approved you.
5. Sign in with your **username or email** plus password. Use **Sign out**
   (bottom-left) when finished.

Forgot your password? Click **“Forgot your password?”** on the sign-in page, enter
your username or email, and follow the reset link (emailed, or shown on screen when
email isn't configured). The link expires after one hour.

> The very first account on a brand-new system becomes an approved administrator
> automatically, so the team can get set up.

### 3. Finding your way around
The dark menu on the left is always visible and split into:
- **Volunteer** — Dashboard, My Availability, Services, Role Swaps, Training, My
  Profile, and this Manual.
- **Administration** *(admins only)* — Admin Home, People, Roles, Trainings,
  Schedule, Availability Grid, Swap Approvals.

Your name and account type show at the bottom with **Sign out**. Coloured banners
at the top of the page confirm actions or warn you.

## For volunteers

### 4. Your dashboard
Your home base shows:
- **Training to complete** — required training you must finish before serving.
- **Your upcoming assignments** — services you're on, with a status badge
  (Scheduled / Confirmed / Declined).
- **Your roles & qualification** — each role and whether you're qualified or still
  in training.
- **Swaps you can cover** — open requests you're qualified to pick up.

### 5. Setting your availability
Open **My Availability**.
1. Choose **✅ Available** or **⛔ Unavailable**.
2. Pick a **From date**. Leave **To date** blank for a single day, or set it to
   mark a whole range at once (every day in the range is saved).
3. Add an optional note and click **Save availability**.

Use **Quick mark upcoming Sundays** to flag the next eight Sundays in one click
each. Your marked dates are listed on the right; **Remove** clears any entry.

### 6. Services & the roster
Open **Services** to see upcoming and recent services. Click **Open** on one to
view its date, time, location, any **service notes**, the **📄 programme flow
document** (if attached), and the full **roster** (who serves in each role).

### 7. Confirming or declining an assignment
On a service you're scheduled for, under **“Your role this service”** you can
**Confirm**, **Decline**, or **Request swap**.

### 8. Role swaps
Can't make a service?
1. On the service page, open **Request swap** under your role, add an optional
   reason, and post it.
2. Qualified teammates see it under **Role Swaps** and click **Volunteer** to cover.
3. An administrator **approves** the swap, reassigning the slot. The status becomes
   **Approved**.

The **Role Swaps** page lists **Swaps you can cover** (only roles you're qualified
for) and **Your swap requests** (with status; cancel one that's still open).

### 9. Service notes
On any service page, the **📝 Notes** panel lets anyone leave a note about that
service. You can delete notes you wrote.

### 10. Training & videos
Open **Training** to see what's assigned and whether it's **To do** or
**Completed**. Click **Start** to open a training, which may include:
- A **🎬 training video** that **automatically pauses the moment you switch tabs or
  leave the window** — so you actually watch it.
- Written content.
- A **“Mark this training complete”** button.

You must complete every required training for a role before you can be scheduled
in it (see §20).

### 11. Your profile
**My Profile** lets you update your name and phone, and shows your roles with their
qualification status. Email and account type are managed by an administrator.

## For administrators *(admins only)*

### 12. Admin home
**Admin Home** gives an overview: counts of people, roles, services, trainings,
open swaps, and training in progress, plus next services and swaps needing
attention.

### 13. Managing people
New sign-ups appear on **Admin Home** and on **People** with a **Pending** badge —
click **Approve** to let them sign in. Open **People**, then **Manage** on anyone to:
- **Approve / revoke approval**, and **mark email verified** manually (or copy the
  verification link to send them).
- **Assign / remove roles** (assigning a role auto-queues its required training).
- **Assign, complete, reset, or remove training.**
- **Make / revoke admin** and **activate / deactivate** the account. (You can't
  change your own admin/approval status.)

### 14. Roles
Open **Roles** to create serving positions (Audio Engineer, Lighting, Camera, …).
Edit a role's name/description or delete it. Each shows how many people are assigned
and how many are fully qualified.

### 15. Building training
Open **Trainings** to create modules. For each set:
- **Title** and short description.
- **Video link** — a YouTube, Vimeo, or direct `.mp4` URL; it embeds for the
  volunteer and auto-pauses when they leave the window.
- **Linked role**.
- **Content / instructions**.
- **Required to serve** — tick if it must be completed before serving in that role.

Edit or delete trainings; each shows completion counts.

### 16. Services & documents
Open **Schedule**. **+ New service** creates a service with **title, date, start
time, location, notes**, and an optional **programme flow document** (PDF, Word,
PowerPoint, Excel, images). Later you can edit any of these — including the time and
location — and attach, replace, or remove the document from the service's page.

### 17. The scheduling board
Click **Schedule** on a service to build its team. For each role, every candidate
shows:
- An availability dot for that date — 🟢 available, 🔴 unavailable, ⚪ not marked.
- A badge: **Qualified** or **Not trained**.

Click **Assign** for a qualified person. Untrained people are blocked, but
**Override** assigns them anyway. The **current roster** at the top lets you
**Remove** anyone.

### 18. Availability grid
**Availability Grid** is a matrix of every volunteer against each upcoming service
date, using the same green / red / grey dots — the fastest way to see who's free.

### 19. Swap approvals
**Swap Approvals** lists all swap requests. When someone has volunteered, the
request shows **Ready to approve** — **Approve** reassigns the slot to them, or
**Reject** declines it.

## Reference

### 20. The qualification rule
The heart of the system: **a volunteer can only be scheduled for a role once they
have completed every required training linked to that role.**
- Assigning a role auto-queues its required training.
- Until it's done, the person shows **“Training X/Y”** and can't be assigned
  (admins may override).
- The same rule decides which open swaps a volunteer may cover.

### 21. Quick FAQ
- **I just registered but can't sign in.** Open the email verification link, then
  wait for an administrator to approve your account — both are required.
- **I can't be assigned to a role.** You likely haven't finished its required
  training — check **Training**.
- **The training video stopped.** Intentional — it pauses when you leave the
  window. Return to the tab and press play.
- **A week off shows many rows.** A range is stored as one entry per day; that's
  normal and lets schedulers see each day.
- **I can't see the admin menu.** Those tools are for administrators; ask an admin
  to grant access from **People**.
- **I forgot to confirm and the service passed.** Confirming is a courtesy so the
  team knows you're coming; it doesn't remove you from the roster.
